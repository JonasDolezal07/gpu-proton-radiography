PURPOSE
-------
The downsampled-field study (run_convergence_grid.py) could only compare
progressively coarser grids against the native 64×64×128 zpinch grid.
It demonstrated that halving the resolution degrades the radiograph by 21%,
but it could not confirm whether the native grid is itself converged.

This study addresses that gap using an analytical test field.


SETUP
-----
Field:        Gaussian Bz filament
              B_z(y, z) = B_max × exp(-(y² + z²) / (2 r₀²))
              x-independent (uniform along beam axis)
              B_max = 1.0 T,  r₀ = 10 mm

              Each resolution is generated independently from the analytical
              formula — not downsampled from a coarser version.  The 256×256
              reference is therefore an independent analytical truth.

              Domain: x ∈ [-50, 50] mm,  y ∈ [-50, 50] mm,  z ∈ [-50, 50] mm
              (same x-span as zpinch, symmetric transverse domain)
              Field is negligible at the domain boundary (e^{-12.5} ≈ 3.7×10⁻⁶
              at y = z = ±50 mm = ±5 r₀)

Physics:      B_z deflects particles in -y (F_y = -qv_xB_z < 0).  Maximum
              on-axis deflection: θ_max = (q/p)×B_max×L_field ≈ 10.4°.
              All 500k particles hit the detector at all resolutions.

NX_BEAM = 8 (field is x-independent; any NX is exact for trilinear interpolation)
NY = NZ = N  (varied; the convergence axis)

Particles:    500,000  monoenergetic parallel beam
              E = 14.7 MeV,  dt = 0.2 ps,  max_steps = 25,000

Reference:    N = 256  (r₀/dx = 25.5 — analytically overresolved)

STRUCTURE OF THE RESULTS
-------------------------

The correlation does not decrease monotonically as resolution coarsens.
There are three distinct regimes:

1. Peak-missing plateau  (N = 4, 8, 16;  r₀/dx = 0.3–1.5,  corr ≈ 0.81–0.83)

   At these resolutions the Gaussian peak is systematically missed.  All
   tested grids have an even number of nodes spanning y ∈ [-50, 50] mm;
   np.linspace(-50, 50, N) for even N never places a node at y = 0.  The
   nearest node to the centre at each resolution is:

     N = 4:   nearest y-node at ±16.7 mm → B_z_peak = 0.249 T  (25% of B_max)
     N = 8:   nearest y-node at  ±7.1 mm → B_z_peak = 0.775 T  (78%)
     N = 16:  nearest y-node at  ±3.3 mm → B_z_peak = 0.946 T  (95%)

   With the peak severely underrepresented, the GPU trilinear sampler
   interpolates a much weaker field at y ≈ 0.  Protons experience only a
   fraction of the true deflection.  The radiograph is dominated by an
   undeflected beam cluster; peak_rel ≈ 0.37–0.40 (peak 60% too low).

   The three grids produce very similar images (corr ≈ 0.82 with each other)
   because they all converge to the same "no peak" limit.  Their moderate
   correlation with the 256×256 reference (0.81–0.83) reflects the fact that
   the background structure (beam body) is similar, not that the physics is
   correct.

2. Transitional anomaly  (N = 32;  r₀/dx = 3.1,  corr = 0.724 — lowest of all)

   At 32×32 the nearest node is ±1.61 mm from y = 0, giving B_z ≈ 0.987 T
   (99% of B_max).  The peak is now nearly captured, but the curvature of
   the Gaussian is poorly resolved: only 3.1 cells span r₀ in each transverse
   direction.  The interpolated field profile is artificially sharp — steeper
   than the true Gaussian in the regions between nodes.

   This creates an over-focusing effect: the caustic is too narrow and too
   bright.  Peak_rel = 2.63 (caustic peak 2.6× too high).  The radiograph
   morphology is wrong in a different way from the peak-missing regime —
   it has a visible but distorted caustic structure — which produces a LOWER
   correlation with the reference than the featureless images at N = 4–16.

   This non-monotonic "transitional dip" at r₀/dx ≈ 3 is a generic artefact
   of trilinear field interpolation on coarse grids: the onset of resolving
   the peak coincides with maximum distortion of the field curvature.

3. Convergence regime  (N = 64, 128;  r₀/dx = 6.3, 12.7)

   At N = 64 the field is adequately resolved: r₀/dx = 6.3 and the
   interpolated Gaussian closely matches the analytical form.  Correlation
   jumps to 0.915 and peak_rel falls to 1.095 (10% high, residual from
   curvature resolution).

   At N = 128 (r₀/dx = 12.7), correlation rises to 0.964.  The remaining
   3.6% gap below the 256×256 reference at 128×128 is consistent with a
   combination of residual field-sampling error (~2%) and statistical noise
   (~1.4% from the particle-count study at 500k particles).


RESIDUAL ERROR AT 64×64 (NATIVE EQUIVALENT)
--------------------------------------------
The native zpinch xy-resolution is 64 cells over 100 mm (dx = 1.587 mm).
For a field feature at scale r₀ = 10 mm, this gives r₀/dx = 6.3 — the same
ratio as the N = 64 analytic test.

The analytic test gives corr = 0.915 at this ratio.  Decomposing the 8.5%
gap from perfect correlation:

  — Statistical noise contribution: ~1.4% (from particle-count study, N=500k)
  — Field-sampling error contribution: ~7.1%
  — Peak fluence error: ~10% (peak_rel = 1.095)

This confirms that for field structures at the r₀ ≈ 10 mm scale (comparable
to the z-pinch radius), the native 64×64 xy grid resolves the gross morphology
but has measurable error in fine caustic structure and peak fluence.

The z-direction of the native zpinch grid is resolved at 128 cells over 80 mm
(dz = 0.630 mm), giving r₀/dz ≈ 16 — closer to the N = 128 performance band
(corr ≈ 0.96).  The xy-resolution is therefore the limiting dimension of the
native zpinch field, consistent with the near-plateau anomaly observed in the
downsampled-field study (8×8×16 and 16×16×32 gave nearly identical results
because z was the bottleneck at those resolutions, but the native z is adequate).


CONVERGENCE CRITERION
---------------------
The results support a practical resolution rule for this class of field:

  r₀/dx ≥ 6   →  gross morphology correct (corr ≈ 0.91), peak ~10% high
  r₀/dx ≥ 12  →  near-noise-floor performance (corr ≈ 0.96)
  r₀/dx ≥ 25  →  analytically converged (reference)

where r₀ is the scale of the smallest feature of physical interest and dx is
the field-grid spacing.  This is equivalent to the visual-inspection rule of
thumb "at least 5–10 grid cells across the minimum feature of interest"
stated in the downsampled-field notes, now quantified with precise thresholds.

Note: the metric includes statistical noise (~1.4% correlation gap for N=500k).
The true field-sampling error at r₀/dx = 6 is ~7%, not 8.5%.


WHAT THIS STUDY ESTABLISHES
-----------------------------
The analytical test confirms two things that the downsampled-field study
could not:

1. The convergence behaviour is monotonic above r₀/dx ≈ 6 — there is no
   anomalous plateau or reversal at finer resolutions.  The native grid is
   on the correct side of the 3.1-cell transitional anomaly.

2. The native 64×64 xy resolution (r₀/dx ≈ 6 for ~10 mm features) is NOT
   converged in the strong sense — there is measurable error relative to
   the analytical truth.  The 8.5% correlation deficit is real field-sampling
   error, not just noise.

The correct paper statement is:

  "The analytical-field convergence test shows that at r₀/dx ≈ 6, consistent
  with the native transverse resolution of the supplied zpinch field, the
  radiograph reproduces gross morphological features with image correlation
  0.915 relative to an analytically overresolved reference.  Doubling the
  transverse resolution to r₀/dx ≈ 13 raises the correlation to 0.964.
  For the zpinch field, fine caustic structure and peak fluence accuracy
  require attention to field-grid resolution; the downsampled-field study
  establishes that a factor-of-2 reduction in xy spacing causes a 21% drop
  in correlation, and the analytic test quantifies the absolute reference."
